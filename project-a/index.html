Jellime

Scientific Name:

Jellime

Discovered by / at:

Polypsia undula

Family:

Digitalis Aquatica — a family of generative marine organisms created through noise-based motion systems.

Habitat:

Deep ocean layers with dim light and gentle underwater currents.

Appearance:

A black soft-bodied organism with 2 eyes and a pulsing, elastic shape that continuously changes size and position. Its form resembles a mix between a jellyfish and a slime.

Key Characteristics:

  • No limbs, but a big tail.
  • Usually black but changes color when approached by others...
  • Appears curious, often watching with its round, reflective eyes.
  • Displays a gentle pulse or “breathing” rhythm while floating.

What we know so far about [Jellime]?
Jellime is a harmless, curious oceanic being that spends its time drifting peacefully through calm waters.
It seems aware of nearby movement, responding with slow, deliberate gestures and gentle pulsing motions,
as if it breathes with the rhythm of the sea itself.
Guidelines for human interaction with this creature:
Approach Jellime slowly and patiently. When it senses your presence,
its eyes widen and change its color into flashing red as a warning sign. It may tilt and drift closer or further.
